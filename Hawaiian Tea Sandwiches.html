With the July heat, many are interested in eating less, eating light. A salad, a sandwich or even just grabbing hor 'doeuvres or "canapes" and tea sandwiches become appealing eating. Canapes can become interesting and fullfilling lunch, brunch and yes - even dinner on a hot muggy summer night.

A canape begins with smaller piece of bread, cracker or pastry component. There is a bottom to this sandwich, but most times, no top. The appetizing part is generally the middle.

Start with bread of your choice. Trim the crusts or even cut the bread using a cookie cutter for interesting shapes. You can even find various breads at the grocers called Cocktail breads that are sliced specifically for canapes.

Now let your imagination run wild. Whatever sounds good on these mini-sandwiches is how to build them. Be sure to check the fridge for leftovers. Many times, the leftover turkey, ham or even baked beans make great canapes. Take some pumpernickle bread, spread a teaspoon full of baked beans on top with a small slice of onion.

Try ham salad, chicken salad, or tuna salad as a base for your canapy, adding a pickle or olive slice is good. Even a chunk of pineapple is good atop this canape.

Cream cheese, cottage cheese, various sliced cheeses add appeal to several ingredients. Even peanut butter and jelly are great on canapes. The kids will think it's great and served with an ice cream shake alongside will make this the hit meal of the week.

Happy Day,
Barb

  • 1 8-oz. pkg. cream cheese
  • 1 15-oz. can crushed pineapple
Drain pineapple very good - press into sieve or wring in cotton towel. Mix softened cream cheese with pineapple. Spread on crackers or small cocktail rye bread.

Canape Ideas

Start with Cocktail Rye, White, Wheat or Pumpernickle bread.

Spread slice of bread lightly with mayonnaise. Top with:

  • Tomato slice and dollop of shrimp salad.
  • Dollop of refried beans and jalepeno pepper
  • Dollop of peanut butter and slice of banana
  • Cucumber slice, tomato slice & bacon crumbles
Spread slice of bread lightly with mustard of your choice. Top with:
  • proscuitto ham slice and olive slice.
  • American cheese slice with slice of hot dog
  • Swiss cheese with slice of corned beef
 

When using a cookie cutter to cut various shapes of bread, slightly freeze the bread first for a cleaner, simplier cut.
