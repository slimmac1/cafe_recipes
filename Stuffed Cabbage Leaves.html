Dear Subscriber,

Do you...Live to eat....or Eat to live.....?

People are beginning to be more concious of their eating habits today. Some, such as diabetics, are forced to eat low fat, less salt and sugar and controlled portions. This style of eating is healthy for everyone, not just diabetics, and more people are becoming ever-so aware of their food intake as well as the process of preparation.

Many of our subscribers have asked for "easy" healthy and diabetic recipes. Here's some tasty and simple ideas following these guidelines.

Fresh vegetables are always acceptable eating and summertime offers us the best. Take some fresh deep green steamed asparagus, sprinkle with sesame seeds. Another colorful and delicious combo is fresh snap beans, steamed with crisp yellow bell pepper julienne strips. Combine 2-Tablespoons olive oil, 2-Tablespoons balsamic vinegar and 2-Tablespoons water. Sprinkle some fresh basil over vegies, then drizzle the dressing over top. Yummy!

Corn-on-the-cob made on the grill is excellent. Peel back the husks. Do not remove husks but discard the silk. Spray with a (fat-free) butter-flavored spray, then sprinkle on some chili powder! Yeah, really - it's delicious! Replace the husks and wrap individual cobs in heavy duty aluminum foil. Grill approximately 25 minutes.

A simple entree is any white fish (hake is good). Sprinkle your white fish fillet with lemon-pepper seasoning, broil until it flakes when you touch it with a fork.

Turkey is a low fat and easy food to prepare. What's nice about turkey is that you can make the dinner feast, but then freeze portions of left over turkey and use for several other combinations of low-fat lunches and dinners.

For dessert, section some pears, sprinkle with a bit of cinnamon and pop in the microwave for about a minute. This is good done with apples as well, but apples need a longer time in micro. Cantaloupe is good this time of year. Add a scoop of yogurt for a terrific dessert.

Eat to live!

Happy Day,
Barb

I make this in the morning and when I get home from work, it's ready to eat.
Submitted by Bea Moseley of Seattle, WA
  • 12 to 14 Large green cabbage leaves
  • 1-1/4 lb. ground turkey breast
  • 1/4 cup egg substitute
  • 1 small onion (minced)
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • 1 Granny Smith apple (chopped)
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 Tablespoon dillweed
  • 2 Tablespoons parsley (fresh or flakes)
  • Pepper (to taste)
    Cooking Sauce
  • 1 Tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 onions (sliced thinly)
  • 1 28-oz. can crushed tomatoes (no-salt added)
  • 3 Tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 Tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon Worchestershire sauce

Blanch cabbage leaves in boiling water for 3 minutes. Drain and refresh under running cold water to stop the cooking process. Drain again on paper towels and set aside.

In a large bowl, combine turkey breast, egg substitute, onion, garlic, apple, cooked rice, dill weed, parsley, and pepper. Mix well.

Place the cabbage leaves on a work surface. Divide the filling equally between the cabbage leaves, putting about 1/4 cup of the turkey-rice mixture at the bottom of each cabbage leaf and roll up, folding in the bottom and sides to enclose the filling. Set filled leaves aside.

In a large nonstick skillet, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic; sauté until onions are limp, about 4 minutes. Stir in remaining sauce ingredients and cook, uncovered, for another 5 minutes. Stir occasionally.

Place half of the sauce in the bottom of a 4-quart or larger slow-cooker. Arrange filled cabbage leaves, seam side down, on top of the sauce, making several layers. Spoon the remaining sauce over the cabbage rolls. Do not stir.

Cover and cook on LOW for 7 to 9 hours, or on HIGH for 3-1/2 to 4 hours.

To serve, transfer cabbage rolls to a large serving platter and top with sauce. Serve at once.

2 Roll-serving: 251 calories Diabetic exchanges: 3 very lean protein, 2 carbohydrate, 1/2 fruit, 3 vegetable, 1/2 bread/starch

 

To slice corn kernels from the cob, use the center "funnel" of a bundt pan to steady the cob while you slice off the kernels. The severed kernels fall neatly into the bowl of the pan.
