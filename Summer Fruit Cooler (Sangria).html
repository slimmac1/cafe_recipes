Recipe of the Week

All sorts of canned fruits make wonderfully refreshing coolers. The beauty of these refreshing beverages is the real fruit flavor without any of the fuss of peeling, pitting, or sprinkling with lemon juice to prevent discoloration. All the work has been done for you, so you can simply sit back on the porch rocker and enjoy.

Ingredients:
2 cans (16 ounces each) sliced or halved apricots, peaches, pears, whole plums, pineapple chunks or fruit cocktail, packed in lite syrup or juice
2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
4 cups sparkling white wine or club soda
Ice cubes
Mint sprigs or edible flowers, such as nasturtiums, optional

Preparation:
Drain the fruit, reserving 1 cup of the syrup or juice. (Plums must be pitted.) If desired, cut and set aside 8 thin slices of fruit for garnish. In a blender or food processor, purée the fruit with the reserved syrup and the lemon juice. Use immediately or chill until ready to use, up to 8 hours.

To assemble the drinks, pour 1/2 cup of the fruit base into each of 8 tall glasses. Gently stir in ½ cup of the wine or soda, then fill the glass with ice cubes. Garnish with reserved fruit and mint sprigs or flower blossoms, if desired. Serve immediately.

Note: This is a barely sweet beverage for sipping by itself or before dinner. If you want to turn it into a dessert “Fruit Float,” use ginger ale or 7-up instead of the club soda and top off with a scoop of ice cream or sherbet.

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Servings: 8

Nutritional Information Per Serving:

72 calories; 0 g fat; 0 mg cholesterol; 302 mg sodium; 19 g carbohydrate; 2 g fiber; 1 g protein.

 

Dried Herbs. The general rule of thumb is one tablespoon of fresh herbs is equivalent to one teaspoon of dried ones
